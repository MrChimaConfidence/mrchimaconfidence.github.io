01 Overview

The Challenge

Standing out in a noisy space

Music promotion is saturated. Every artist is fighting for the same eyeballs across Instagram, YouTube, and streaming platforms. Paperboy Music needed visuals that could stop the scroll — not blend into it.

The Approach

Bold type, strong contrast

Rather than following trends, the design system leans into raw typographic power and intentional contrast — creating a visual language that feels immediate, confident, and authentically the artist's own.

The Scope

Identity across every surface

From social media graphics and cover art to promotional banners, the work spans every touch-point where the music meets its audience — maintaining consistency without sacrificing energy.

The Outcome

A visual voice that resonates

The final system gives Paperboy Music a strong, memorable presence. Every asset communicates the same energy — so the brand is instantly recognisable whether someone sees it on a phone screen or a billboard.

03 Design Process
01

Discovery & Brand Direction

Before opening Figma, I spent time understanding the music itself, its genre, energy, and the audience it speaks to. The visual direction had to feel native to the sound, not just aesthetically pleasing in isolation. Reference boards were built around rawness, confidence, and contrast.

02

Typography as Personality

Type is the backbone of the identity. The selections prioritise weight and hierarchy, pairing an expressive display face with a clean secondary typeface. The combination reads as bold without feeling loud, and gives every asset an immediate sense of authorship.

03

Colour & Contrast System

A deliberately restricted palette ensures consistency and punch. High contrast is the primary tool, using light and dark to direct the eye and create compositions that hold up at any size, from a tiny phone notification to a large-format print.

04

Scalable Asset System

Each design was built as a template, not a one-off. This means every future release, event, or campaign can be turned around quickly while staying on-brand. The system scales without needing to reinvent the wheel each time.

04 Outcome

Visuals that
make you
stop scrolling

The final set of assets gives Paperboy Music a strong, cohesive visual presence that translates across platforms without losing energy or identity. The work demonstrates that visual design for music isn't decoration — it's an extension of the sound itself.

Consistency across every touch-point means audiences immediately recognise the brand, building familiarity and trust over time. The scalable system ensures future releases can match this quality without starting from scratch.
